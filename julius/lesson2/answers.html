Part A — Perimeter & Area (Problems 1–9)

10 pts
#ProblemAnswerKey Work / Explanation
1 Perimeter of 18×12 ft rectangle L1 60 ft P = 2(18 + 12) = 2(30) = 60 ft
2 Area of triangle, b=14 cm, h=9 cm L1 63 cm² A = ½ × 14 × 9 = ½ × 126 = 63 cm²  ⚠️ Watch: forgetting the ½
3 Area of parallelogram, b=11 m, h=7 m L1 77 m² A = bh = 11 × 7 = 77 m²  ⚠️ Watch: using slant side instead of height
4 Area of trapezoid, b₁=8 in, b₂=14 in, h=6 in L2 66 in² A = ½(8 + 14) × 6 = ½ × 22 × 6 = ½ × 132 = 66 in²
5 Perimeter of 6-sided polygon (sides: 100, 40, 40, 60, 60, 60 cm) L2 360 cm P = 100 + 40 + 40 + 60 + 60 + 60 = 360 cm  ⚠️ Watch: missing a side
6a Area of right triangle, legs 5 ft & 12 ft L2 30 ft² A = ½ × 5 × 12 = 30 ft² (legs ARE the base and height)
6b Perimeter of same right triangle L2 30 ft Hypotenuse: √(5²+12²) = √(25+144) = √169 = 13 ft  → P = 5+12+13 = 30 ft
7 Area of L-shape (4×3 top + 10×4 bottom) L2 52 m² Piece 1 (top rect): 4×3 = 12 m²  |  Piece 2 (bottom rect): 10×4 = 40 m²  → Total = 52 m²
8 12×10 room minus 4×4 closet L2 104 ft² Big rect: 12×10 = 120 ft²  −  Closet: 4×4 = 16 ft²  = 104 ft²
9 Rectangle (10×6) + triangle (b=6, h=5) L3 75 cm² Rect: 10×6 = 60 cm²  +  Tri: ½×6×5 = 15 cm²  = 75 cm²

Part C — ELA: "The Grid That Grew" (Q1–Q6)

6 pts
Q#SkillAnswerWhy This Answer / Tutor Explanation
Q1 Main Idea B Every paragraph connects back to the rectangular grid and geometry — not just history. (A) is too narrow; (C) is a detail; (D) is an overgeneralization not supported by the text.
Q2 Text Structure A The passage moves chronologically (1811 plan → critics → adjustments → today) AND contrasts Manhattan grid vs. old neighborhoods. Both structures are present. ⚠️ Watch: (B) problem-solution is too simple — the passage never fully "solves" a problem.
Q3 Signal Words B "As a result" = cause-effect signal. ¶2 explains the geometric properties of a rectangle → the result is easy calculation. (A) is wrong — "As a result" never signals contrast.
Q4 Supporting Detail C Directly stated in ¶3: "left no room for diagonal routes, making it inefficient to travel across the city at an angle." (B) is the opposite of what the text says.
Q5 Inference B ¶5 says irregular shapes make it "much harder to estimate distances or areas at a glance" — this implies people use geometric reasoning (grids = predictable math) to navigate. ⚠️ (A), (C), (D) are not supported by the text.
Q6 Author's Purpose C ¶6 zooms out from the 1811 plan to say geometry shapes how "millions of people move through space every day" — it elevates the topic from history to lived human experience. (A), (B), (D) misread the paragraph's function.
