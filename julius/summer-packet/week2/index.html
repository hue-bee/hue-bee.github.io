Reading — Informational Passage

📖 Read the passage once for understanding. Annotate: circle signal words, underline the main idea of each paragraph. Answer all 5 questions.

[¶1] The circle is, without question, the most mathematically efficient shape in engineering. Its single defining property — that every point on its boundary lies exactly the same distance from the center — makes it uniquely suited to problems of pressure, rotation, and coverage. A water pipe is circular, not square, because a circular cross-section carries the maximum volume of fluid for the minimum amount of pipe material. A gear is circular because only a circle can transmit rotational force smoothly and without skipping.

[¶2] However, the circle is not always practical. A circular room wastes corner space; a circular box cannot be stacked efficiently. As a result, engineers and architects must constantly choose between the mathematical ideal and the physical reality. A bridge cable is circular in cross-section, but the roadway it supports is rectangular. A satellite dish is a circle, but the building it sits on is a box. Each shape is chosen for a specific reason.

[¶3] The most dramatic examples of the circle’s power appear at extremes of scale. The Large Hadron Collider — the world’s largest particle accelerator — is a circle 27 kilometers in circumference, because only a circular path allows particles to be accelerated repeatedly without losing them. At the other extreme, the lens of the human eye is a circle because the pupil must expand and contract symmetrically. The circle works because of its symmetry: every radius is the same length, and that equality distributes force, pressure, and light perfectly.

[¶4] Students who learn to calculate the circumference and area of a circle are not merely memorizing formulas. They are learning a mode of reasoning — from center to edge, from a single measurement to a prediction about the whole boundary. Every time an engineer specifies a pipe diameter, a chef selects a cake pan, or a scientist adjusts a telescope mirror, the same two formulas are at work: C = 2πr and A = πr².
