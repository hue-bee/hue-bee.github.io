Unit Conversion Problems

2 pts
#ProblemAnswerKey Work / Explanation
1 Swimming pool is 27 feet long. Convert to yards. L2 9 yards Rule: 1 yard = 3 feet — going UP from a smaller unit (feet) to a larger unit (yards) means divide
27 ft ÷ 3 = 9 yards
Check: 9 yards × 3 ft/yd = 27 ft ✓
2 Relay race is 2.5 miles long, 4 runners per team, each running an equal segment. How many feet does each runner cover? L4 3,300 feet Rule: 1 mile = 5,280 feet — convert total distance to feet first, then divide by number of runners
Step 1: 2.5 × 5,280 = 13,200 total feet
Step 2: 13,200 ÷ 4 = 3,300 feet per runner
⚠️ SHSAT Trap: students who stop after Step 1 and answer "13,200 ft" forgot the question asks for the distance per runner, not the total

ELA — Reading: Text Structure

5 pts
Q#SkillAnswerWhy This Answer / Tutor Explanation
Q1 Organizational Structure L2 C The passage sets up a problem (conversion errors can cause pipelines to burst) and presents a solution (dimensional analysis). (A) sequence is present but not the primary structure — the passage isn't a step-by-step process narrative. (B) compare/contrast is only a small detail in ¶2. (D) description undersells the cause-effect/problem-solution framing that runs through all four paragraphs.
⚠️ Trap: students who pick (A) because ¶3 lists steps — that's a supporting detail, not the passage's overall structure.
Q2 Supporting Detail L2 C ¶1 states the smallest lines "deliver exact quantities to each building — often measured in fractions of a gallon per second." (A) describes the largest mains, not the smallest lines. (B) describes the mid-size branches. (D) is a detail from ¶2 about pressure, unrelated to pipe size.
Q3 Signal Word Meaning L3 B "In contrast to" signals that the two things being compared are different in a specific way — here, a conversion error has a far worse consequence (a burst pipe) than an ordinary calculation slip. (A) reverses the comparison. (C) and (D) are unsupported claims not stated in the text.
⚠️ Trap: (A) is tempting because both are "errors," but the text explicitly says conversion errors are the more dangerous kind.
Q4 Signal Words L3 C "However careful the calculation…" signals a contrast — no matter how careful you are, the sanity check is still never skipped. (A) "precisely why" signals cause/explanation, not contrast. (B) signals consistency/routine. (D) is simply a descriptive phrase about the method, not a signal word.
Q5 Central Idea / Extended Example L4 Open response — model answer below Model answer: "The author argues that dimensional analysis is a universal problem-solving tool used far beyond engineering, as shown by the phrase 'The bridge between a measurement you have and a measurement you need is always a conversion fact.' The swimmer, baker, and driver examples show that anyone converting between units — laps to miles, cups to milliliters, kilometers to dollars — is using the same reasoning as the water-system engineers in ¶1–¶3."
Award credit for any response that (1) names the central idea — dimensional analysis applies broadly, not just to engineering — and (2) quotes one exact supporting phrase from ¶4.
⚠️ Trap: students who only summarize the swimmer/baker/driver examples without stating the broader central idea should be redirected to "zoom out" — what does this comparison prove about the whole passage?
