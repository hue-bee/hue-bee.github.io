Problem 1 L4

Walls + Ceiling · Round UP

Identify which faces to include. Calculate each face separately. Divide total area by coverage and round UP.

A gym is 30 ft long, 20 ft wide, and 12 ft tall. You will paint the 4 walls and the ceiling only — not the floor. Each can of paint covers 75 ft². How many cans do you need?

Cans needed: cans

Problem 2 L4

Open Top · Subtract Circle

No lid — skip the top face. A circular hole is cut into the bottom — subtract that area from the bottom face.

An open-top fish tank has l = 24 in, w = 12 in, h = 16 in. A circular drain with radius 1.5 in is cut into the bottom. How many square inches of glass are needed? Leave π in your answer.

Glass needed: in²

Problem 3 L4

4 Walls Only · Round UP

Walls only — no floor, no ceiling. Divide total wall area by roll coverage. Fractional rolls still need a full roll!

A bedroom is 15 ft long, 12 ft wide, and 9 ft tall. You will wallpaper the 4 walls only — not the floor or ceiling. Each roll covers 40 ft². How many rolls do you need?

Rolls needed: rolls
🏖

Problem 4 L4

Open Top · Subtract Circle

Same pattern as Problem 2 — no lid, circular hole in the bottom. Watch the difference: this time you're given the diameter, not the radius.

An open-top sandbox has l = 18 in, w = 10 in, h = 6 in. A circular drainage hole with diameter 4 in is cut into the bottom. How many square inches of wood are needed? Leave π in your answer.

Wood needed: in²
🧩

Problem 5 L4

Multi-Twist · Read Carefully

This problem has TWO twists. Map all the faces that exist first — then calculate. Read the question at the end carefully before answering.

A storage shed is 20 ft long, 10 ft wide, and 10 ft tall. The shed has no floor (it sits on concrete) and one wall is left open as an entrance (one of the 20 ft × 10 ft walls). You will paint all remaining surfaces. Each can covers 60 ft². How many cans do you need?
