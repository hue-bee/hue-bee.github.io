What is L4?

Why Are These Problems Harder?

🟢
L2 Problems
One formula, one step. Plug in the numbers and go. The question tells you exactly what to find.
🔴
L4 Problems
Multiple steps + a twist. You must decide which faces to count, what to add or subtract, and sometimes round up.
🧠 The L4 Pattern — Every Time
  1. Identify which surfaces actually exist (open tops? missing floors? holes?)
  2. Calculate each face's area using the right formula
  3. Add or subtract depending on what the problem asks
  4. Answer the real question (cans needed? inches of glass?)

Toolkit

Everything You Need — One Page

📦 Box Formula (Full)
SA = 2lw + 2lh + 2wh
Think of 3 pairs of matching faces: Top/bottom (lw), Front/back (lh), Left/right (wh).
🔵 Circle Formulas
A = πr²
r = d ÷ 2
You need this when a circular hole is cut into a face.
⚡ Open Top / Open Bottom Rule
No lid = subtract one lw face. No floor = subtract another. Count what's actually there.
Problem 10 · L4

🎨 Painting a Room — Read It Like a Detective

The Problem
A room is 14 ft long, 10 ft wide, 8 ft tall. Paint the 4 walls and the ceiling only — not the floor. Each can covers 50 ft². How many cans?
🕵️ What Faces Exist?
✅ Ceiling (lw = 14 × 10)
✅ Wall 1 & Wall 2 (lh = 14 × 8) × 2
✅ Wall 3 & Wall 4 (wh = 10 × 8) × 2
❌ Floor — skip it!
Before you calculate: how many faces will you add up? What formula do you start with?
Problem 10 · Step-by-Step

🎨 Solving the Room — Four Clean Steps

Step 1 · Ceiling
Ceiling = l × w
14 × 10 = 140 ft²
Step 2 · Long Walls
2 long walls = 2 × l × h
2 × 14 × 8 = 224 ft²
Step 3 · Short Walls
2 short walls = 2 × w × h
2 × 10 × 8 = 160 ft²
Step 4 · Total Area
Total = 140 + 224 + 160
= 524 ft²
⚡ The L4 Twist — Round UP!
Cans = 524 ÷ 50 = 10.48

You can't buy 0.48 of a can. Round up to 11 cans.

Always round UP for "how many cans/rolls/tiles" — you can't have partial supplies.
🪤 Common Trap
Students use SA = 2lw + 2lh + 2wh and include the floor. Read the problem: 4 walls + ceiling only.
Problem 12 · L4

🐟 The Fish Tank — Two Twists in One

The Problem
Open-top tank: l = 24 in, w = 12 in, h = 16 in. A circular drain with radius 1.5 in is cut into the bottom. How many square inches of glass are needed?
🕵️ What Exists? What's Missing?
❌ No lid (open top) — skip lw on top
✅ Bottom face (lw) — but has a hole!
✅ 2 long sides (lh) × 2
✅ 2 short sides (wh) × 2
➖ Circular drain cut out of bottom
If you cut a hole in a piece of glass, how do you find the remaining glass area?
Problem 12 · Step-by-Step

🐟 Solving the Tank — Subtract the Hole

Step 1 · Bottom Face
Bottom = l × w
24 × 12 = 288 in²
Step 2 · Long Sides
2 long sides = 2 × l × h
2 × 24 × 16 = 768 in²
Step 3 · Short Sides
2 short sides = 2 × w × h
2 × 12 × 16 = 384 in²
Step 4 · Open-Top SA
288 + 768 + 384 = 1,440 in²
⚡ The L4 Twist — Subtract the Circle!
Drain area = πr² = π(1.5)² = 2.25π in²

Glass needed = 1440 − 2.25π in²
Glass = 1440 − 2.25π in²
🧠 Why Subtract?
The circular hole is empty — no glass fills it. So we take the full bottom area and remove the circle. Glass = solid area only.
🪤 Common Trap
Forgetting to subtract the drain. Also: the problem says radius = 1.5, not diameter. Don't halve it again!
The Pattern

Every L4 Surface Area Problem — Same 4 Moves

🗺️
1. MAP the faces
List every face that exists. Check for open tops, open bottoms, and special constraints.
🧮
2. CALCULATE each
Use the right formula for each face. Rectangles: l×w. Circles: πr².
➕➖
3. ADD or SUBTRACT
Add all present faces. Subtract holes, drains, or excluded faces.
🎯
4. ANSWER the question
Cans? Round UP. Exact glass? Leave in π. Read what they actually asked.
Problem 10 Pattern
Map (4 walls + ceiling) → Calculate each → Add → Divide by 50 → Round UP
Problem 12 Pattern
Map (no top) → Calculate all sides → Add → Subtract π(1.5)² for drain → Answer in π
