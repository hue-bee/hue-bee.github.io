## 2. Guiding Principle for the 4-Session Plan

With only four one-hour sessions currently available, the plan follows a triage approach. It does
NOT attempt to cover every weak Math domain, because thin coverage across many topics produces no
durable gains. Instead, the four hours concentrate on the highest-return topics — those where Oren
scores near zero, that are quick to teach, and that appear frequently on the SHSAT.

Each topic was selected using three filters:

- **Sort by score, not curriculum order.** The bottom five topics (Measurements, Geometry,
  Percents, Factors, Data) are where points are actually being lost.
- **Weight by test frequency.** Geometry and Percents appear many times on the exam, so small
  percentage gains translate into several raw-score points.
- **Weight by teachability in 60 minutes.** Measurements and Percents are procedural and can be
  substantially improved in a single focused hour.

Oren's existing strengths — Word Problems (75%) and Probability (67%) — are deliberately left out,
since spending scarce hours there would yield little return.

## 3. The Four Sessions

Each hour uses a **50-minute work block + 10-minute break/debrief** structure and follows the same
rhythm: teach -> model -> practice -> check.

### Session 1 — Measurements (the 0% emergency)
Scheduled first because Oren answered zero measurement questions correctly, making this a
foundational gap rather than review. Left untreated, it will keep costing guaranteed points.

### Session 2 — Geometry (18% -> biggest raw-point pool)
Scheduled second because it is both very weak and one of the most frequent Math topics, giving it
the largest single block of recoverable points.

### Session 3 — Percents (29% -> high-frequency, fully procedural)
Scheduled third because percents are high-frequency, entirely rule-based, and ideal for
single-hour mastery.

### Session 4 — Factors/Multiples + Data/Statistics + Cumulative Review
The final hour is a combined high-value cleanup and retention session, since both remaining topics
are quick and review is essential to lock in Sessions 1-3.

## 4. What the 4-Session Plan Deliberately Excludes

Four hours cannot responsibly cover everything, so the following were consciously deferred:

- **Algebra (43%), Ratios (50%), Arithmetic (50%), Inequalities (40%):** mid-range weaknesses, not
  emergencies; partial mastery means a lower point-per-hour return than the near-zero topics.
- **Word Problems and Probability:** already strengths; spending sessions here would be wasteful.

Between sessions, Oren should independently complete his assigned Ascent Math homework
(Absolute Value, Scientific Notation, Factors/Radicals, Coordinates, Imaginary Operations,
Plugins) so that tutoring time is reserved for concepts he cannot self-teach.

### Expected Impact
Concentrating four hours on the four lowest-scoring, highest-frequency topics targets roughly the
12-15 questions Oren is currently missing across Measurements, Geometry, Percents, Factors, and
Data. Recovering even half of those would add meaningful raw points to his 222 Math scaled score
and represents the most efficient use of a limited engagement.

## 5. Long-Term Math Plan (Beyond the First Four Sessions)

If the family is able to extend the engagement, the following phased roadmap builds on the initial
four sessions in order of decreasing point-per-hour return.

### Phase 1 (Sessions 1-4) — Critical Triage — COMPLETED ABOVE
Measurements, Geometry, Percents, Factors/Data. Stops the largest, fastest point losses.

### Phase 2 (Sessions 5-8) — Mid-Range Consolidation
Targets the medium-priority topics where Oren has partial mastery and can be pushed to reliability.

- Session 5 — Algebra: solving linear equations, expressions, substitution (Q59, Q86, Q91, Q108).
- Session 6 — Inequalities: solving, graphing, and the sign-flip rule (Q65, Q107).
- Session 7 — Ratios / Proportions: setting up and solving proportions, scaling (Q66, Q69, Q81).
- Session 8 — Arithmetic & Coordinate Geometry: fluency, negative numbers, plotting and slope.

### Phase 3 (Sessions 9-12) — Strategy, Timing, and Full-Length Practice
Shifts from content gaps to test-taking performance under real conditions.

- Session 9 — Timed drills and pacing: learning when to skip, flag, and return.
- Session 10 — Strategy for hardest question types: back-solving from answer choices, plugging in
  numbers, estimation to eliminate.
- Session 11 — Full-length timed Math section under exam conditions, followed by error analysis.
- Session 12 — Targeted review of the newest error log, plus a second timed section to confirm gains.

### Ongoing Habits Throughout All Phases

- **Error log:** for every missed problem, record the topic, the specific mistake (concept vs.
  careless), and the correct method. This is the single most effective tool for a student whose
  errors cluster by topic.
- **Weekly mixed quiz:** 5 problems drawn from current plus prior topics to force retention.
- **Homework accountability:** complete all assigned Ascent Math homework independently between
  sessions.
- **Periodic remock:** take a fresh full-length mock every 4-6 sessions to measure real progress
  against the ~496-518 cutoff range for target schools.
