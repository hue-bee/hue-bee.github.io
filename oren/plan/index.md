## 3. How the Summer Concepts Map Into the Plan

The Ascent summer schedule provides the skills spine and is used as the **sequencing guide**, while the actual content is swapped to match what Oren's diagnostics require.

- **ELA grammar strand** (Pronoun Clarity → Modifiers → Tenses/Moods → Possessives → Confused Words → Logical Comparison → Subordination/Coordination → Punctuation) forms the backbone of the weekly ELA minutes and directly supports his Revising/Editing growth.
- **Math strand** (Absolute Value → Scientific Notation → Factors/Multiples/Exponents/Radicals → Coordinates → advanced operations → Plug-ins) is used as a touchpoint, but the topics his mocks flag hardest — negatives/absolute value, factors/exponents, percents, and geometry — are front-loaded and expanded rather than followed literally.

## 4. Weekly Session Structure

Each 60-minute session follows a consistent rhythm so it becomes automatic:

- **10 min** — Warm-up review of last week's error log
- **35 min** — Math core
- **12 min** — ELA target
- **3 min** — Assign homework

A recurring **error log** is the engine of the plan. Every missed question is categorized by topic each week, and the warm-up revisits it, so progress is measured against Oren's own data.

## 6. What Mastery Looks Like in Math (the Weakest Area)

Because Math carries this plan, by late October Oren should be able to:

- **Percents:** Fluently set up and solve "what percent," "percent of," and percent-change problems, including two-step discount-then-tax problems, without a calculator — targeting 70%+ from the current ~29%.
- **Negatives, fractions, decimals:** Perform all four operations on signed fractions and decimals accurately and quickly, since these underlie algebra and word problems and are his most frequent silent point-losers.
- **Geometry:** Recall and apply core formulas (area/perimeter of triangles and rectangles, area/circumference of circles, volume of prisms/cylinders, angle relationships) on sight — the biggest single-topic recovery opportunity given the 18% Mock 2 score.
- **Factors/exponents:** Compute GCF/LCM, prime-factorize, and apply exponent and radical rules, lifting the stuck-at-33% Factors/Multiples line.
- **Strategy layer:** Use plug-in and back-solving techniques on algebra and word problems to convert partial understanding into correct answers under time pressure.
