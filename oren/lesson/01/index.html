Habit-Building

The Show-Your-Work Routine

Why this matters for you
You already know this content well. Your score plateau came from working fast and messily, then not trusting or re-checking the answer — not from missing concepts.
The three-step check
1. Rewrite double signs before computing. 2. Circle the sign of the final answer before finalizing the number. 3. Plug the answer back into the original expression as a sanity check.
🤔 Make It Yours
Pick any problem from today. Ask yourself: "How would I know, just by looking at my work, if I made a sign error?" Write your own personal checklist in your own words — it sticks better than one someone hands you.
Habit-Building · Watch It Work

The Routine, Step by Step

DEMOLevel 3
−8 − (−15) + (−6) × 2 = ?
Step 1 · Rewrite Double Signs
Before computing anything, rewrite every double sign in plain form:
−8 + 15 + (−6) × 2
Subtracting a negative became adding — the trap is skipped before it can happen.
Step 2 · Circle the Final Sign
Multiply first (order of operations): (−6) × 2 = −12. Now combine: −8 + 15 − 12 = −5.
Answer: −5
Circle the negative sign on the final answer before writing the number down — this is the moment most sign errors get locked in unnoticed.
Step 3 · Plug Back In
Substitute −5 back into a simplified version of the original relationship to sanity-check the size and sign make sense together.
−8 −(−15) + (−12) = 7 − 12 = −5 ✓
Matches — the answer survives its own check.
🤔 Now You Try Narrating It
Go back to problem 6 or 7 from earlier and narrate your own three steps out loud: what did you rewrite, what sign did you circle, and how did you plug back in to check? If you skipped a step mentally, redo it on paper — that's the exact moment errors hide.
Signed Numbers

What Does a Negative Actually Mean?

🤔 Think About It First
If temperature drops from 3° to −4°, how many degrees did it fall? Try to show that as one arrow on a number line before reading on.

If you got 7, can you explain why — without writing an equation? That instinct is exactly what we're about to turn into a rule.

Now flip it: if the temperature rises from −4° to 3°, is the arrow the same length as before? Does the direction change anything about the distance traveled?

One more: what is the distance between −4° and −4° itself? Why does that answer make sense using the "how far from zero" idea?
The reframe to land
A negative sign is not "a scary math symbol" — it's a direction. Absolute value strips away the direction and asks only: "how far from zero?" So |−4| = 4 and |4| = 4 — same distance, opposite direction.
Building the Rule

Discover the Rule Yourself

🤔 Work Through This
1. If you owe $5 and then owe $3 more, what's your total? → −5 + (−3)
2. If you owe $5 but then earn $3, are you still in debt? By how much? → −5 + 3
3. So when the signs match, what happens to the sizes? When they don't match?
4. What does subtracting a negative actually undo?
5. If you multiply two debts together, (−5) × (−3), does the sign make sense as "a debt of a debt," or do you need a different mental model?
6. Why does multiplying by an odd number of negatives always land negative, but an even number always lands positive? Test it with three negatives, then four.
State the rule in your own words
  • Same signs added → add the sizes, keep the sign.
  • Different signs added → subtract smaller size from larger, keep the "bigger" sign.
  • Odd number of negatives multiplied/divided → answer is negative; even number → positive.
  • Subtracting a negative = adding its opposite: a − (−b) = a + b
✅ Check yourself
Answer the four questions above and write your own version of the rule before checking it against the card on the left. Your wording matters more than memorizing this exact phrasing.

Levels 2–3

Stack the Operations

5Level 2
8 − (−5) − 12 = ?
6Level 3
−3 × (−2) × (−5) = ?
7Level 3
|−6 + 2| − |4 − 9| = ?
⚠️ SHSAT Trap
Problem 7 tempts students to distribute absolute value across the subtraction, i.e. treat |−6+2|−|4−9| like |(−6+2)−(4−9)|. Absolute value bars are grouping symbols — simplify fully inside each pair before doing anything outside it.
🤔 Push Further
For problem 6: does the order you multiply the three negatives in matter? Try grouping them a different way and check the answer stays the same.
For problem 7: what would change if the bars were removed completely — would the answer change, and does that tell you why absolute value matters here?
Level 4 — SHSAT Style

Multi-Step, Test-Day Format

8Level 4

🤔 Before You Start
What does "rises" do to elevation — add or subtract? What does "descends 3 times that distance" mean in numbers, before you touch the sign at all? Translate the words into a signed expression first, then compute.

After you pick an answer: which wrong choice would you get if you forgot to multiply the 45 by 3 first? Which wrong choice comes from mixing up which direction is "down"?
Pronoun Clarity & Agreement

Every Pronoun Needs a Clear Owner

9Level 2

10Level 3
Which sentence fixes the unclear pronoun reference? "Maria told her sister that she had won the scholarship."
🤔 One Question That Solves Both
Point to the exact word each pronoun replaces. If you can't point to exactly one word, the sentence has a problem. Try this test on "Neither... their/his" and on "she" in the Maria sentence — the errors should become visible.

Follow-up: is "Neither of the coaches" singular or plural in the SHSAT's eyes? Why does that matter for choosing "his" over "their"? For the Maria sentence, could you rewrite it a completely different way that avoids the ambiguous pronoun altogether?
