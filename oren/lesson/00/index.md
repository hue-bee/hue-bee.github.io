## 3. Math Diagnostic Set (0:12–0:40)

Hue sequences these easy-to-hard and does *not* correct mid-problem — he notes the process, then discusses. The goal is to see whether the mock weaknesses are real and whether they are conceptual or careless.

- **Signed numbers (Week 1 preview):** −7 + (−4); −3 × (−6); 5 − (−2). Watch for sign confusion, Oren's most frequent silent point-loser.
- **Fractions/decimals:** 2/3 − 1/4; convert 0.375 to a fraction. Tests foundational gaps that underlie algebra.
- **Percents (top target):** "What is 15% of 80?" then "A $60 item is discounted 25% — new price?" This is the chronically-weak, high-frequency zone (28.6% both mocks).
- **Geometry (top target):** area of a triangle (base 10, height 6); area of a circle (r = 5). Geometry regressed to 18.2% on Mock 2, so Hue confirms whether it is formula recall or setup.
- **Word problem (relative strength):** a two-step rate problem — used to end on a confidence win, since word problems sit at 75%.

For each miss, Hue categorizes the cause on the spot: **concept gap, formula not recalled, arithmetic slip, or misread.** That categorization is exactly what the error log will track weekly.

## 4. ELA Diagnostic Set (0:40–0:52)

- **Reading (one short passage):** a **Main Idea** question (his weakest recurring type at 25%) plus one Detail question (a strength) to contrast. Hue prefers a Fiction excerpt, since fiction (27–44%) lags nonfiction.
