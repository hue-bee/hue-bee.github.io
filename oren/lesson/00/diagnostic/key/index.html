P1
Level 1
−7 + (−4) = ?-11Adding a negative means move left on the number line. Start at −7 and go 4 more units left: −8, −9, −10, −11. The key idea is that two negatives do not cancel when you are adding; they combine into a larger negative.
P2
Level 2
−3 × (−6) = ?18A negative times a negative becomes positive. Since 3 × 6 = 18, the value of −3 × (−6) is +18. This is a classic sign-rule check: multiplication of matching signs gives a positive product.
P4
Level 2
2/3 − 1/4 = ?5/12Use a common denominator of 12. Rewrite 2/3 as 8/12 and 1/4 as 3/12, then subtract: 8/12 − 3/12 = 5/12. The denominators stay the same; only the numerators subtract.
P5
Level 3
375/1000 → simplify by GCF3/8Write 0.375 as 375/1000 because the 5 is in the thousandths place. Then reduce by dividing top and bottom by 125: 375 ÷ 125 = 3 and 1000 ÷ 125 = 8, so the fraction is 3/8. A decimal-to-fraction problem is really a place-value problem first, then a simplification problem.

Level 2
What is 15% of 80?12Convert 15% to 0.15 and multiply by 80: 0.15 × 80 = 12. You can also split 15% into 10% + 5%; 10% of 80 is 8 and 5% of 80 is 4, so together that is 12.
P7
Level 4
A $60 item is discounted 25%. What is the new price ?$45First find the discount: 25% of 60 is 0.25 × 60 = 15. Then subtract the discount from the original price: 60 − 15 = 45. The trap is stopping at 15 even though the question asks for the new price, not the amount taken off.
P8
Level 2
Find the area of a triangle with base 10 and height 6.30 square unitsUse the triangle area formula A = 1/2 × b × h. Substitute b = 10 and h = 6: A = 1/2 × 10 × 6 = 30. A common error is forgetting the 1/2 and giving 60, which would be the area of the matching rectangle, not the triangle.
P9
Level 3
Find the area of a circle with radius 5. Leave your answer in terms of π.25π square unitsUse the circle area formula A = πr². With r = 5, you get A = π(5²) = 25π. Because the problem says to leave the answer in terms of π, do not multiply by 3.14.

Q11
Level 2
Choose the correct pronoun: "Every student must bring ___ own calculator."B — his or herThe subject “Every student” is singular, so a formally matching pronoun must also be singular. “His or her” agrees with the singular subject, while “their” is common in everyday speech but is not the best traditional grammar answer on many standardized tests. “They’re” means “they are,” and “our” does not match the subject.
Q12
Level 3
Which sentence fixes the misplaced modifier? "Running down the street, the bus almost hit the boy."B — Running down the street, the boy was almost hit by the bus.The opening phrase “Running down the street” must describe the word that comes right after it. In choice B, the boy is the one running, so the modifier is attached correctly. In the original sentence, it sounds as if the bus was running down the street, which creates the misplaced modifier.
Q13
Level 4
What is the main idea of this passage?B — A coach's calm encouragement helps Maya regain focus and composure after failingThis choice captures the whole passage, not just one detail. Maya misses, the coach calmly resets her thinking, and she succeeds by returning to her routine. Choice A is too absolute because she clearly had just missed three shots, and the other answers distort the coach’s tone or the passage’s purpose.
